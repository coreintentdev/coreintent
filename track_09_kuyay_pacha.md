# Track 09 — Kuyay Pacha (Time of Loving)

**Lane:** Quechua × te reo MI pillar (Indigenous × Indigenous)
**Tempo:** 80 BPM
**Mood:** Andean-electronic, high-altitude, ceremonial-tender

---

## Lesson note (what this track teaches)
This is an **Indigenous × Indigenous** pairing — Quechua + te reo Māori, no European pivot language in the lyric. The lesson here: **the te reo pillar isn't a colonial bridge.** When you pair two Indigenous languages directly, both get to be primary. The track also pairs traditional Andean instruments (charango, quena, zampoña) with modern electronic production (sub-bass, granular pads) — the "Andean electronica" lineage of artists like Nicola Cruz, Chancha Via Circuito, El Búho. The political weight of this pairing is real and worth honoring; don't treat it as exotic flavoring.
