# Track 08 — Sapucaia (Brazil Nut Tree)

**Tempo:** 92 BPM
**Mood:** bossa-MPB, river-shade, generous melancholy

---

## Lesson note (what this track teaches)
Brazilian Portuguese has **soft sibilance** (carioca "sh" for final S), nasalized vowels (ão, em, im), and a **bossa rhythm** that lives between 88-94 BPM. The lesson here: **don't over-instrument bossa.** Most Suno tracks fail by stacking too many instruments — bossa is nylon-string guitar + voice + brushed kit + maybe one melodic instrument. Resist the lift; let the song breathe. Also: titles named after a tree/place root the song in geography, which Suno responds to better than abstract titles.
