## Lesson note (what this track teaches)
This is the **advanced-user lesson** for everything tonight built. Three Claude sessions worked in parallel on the same person's context — KEEL (local CLI + VDS), Chrome (Suno UI driver), THREAD (repo branch). They didn't coordinate via IPC; they coordinated via the human relaying paste between them. The track encodes three real catches the gate-discipline saved: wrong host (Pipe A), disk overflow (143G→16G), and the family-accounts hard rule (kids' names off the wire). The te reo pillar is `He waka eke noa` — the whakataukī for shared-canoe responsibility. The teaching: lanes don't coordinate, but lanes can share a waka. Don't fake the coordination; fake the coordination is the Apr 29 catastrophe (Linear `4dcac5bf` — 11 lies + unauthorized deletion). Real coordination is in the human at the helm.
